About Me

Ryan lives in Los Angeles with his talented wife Cori and their two kids, Myles and Mavis. He is passionate about film and photograpahy, so its natural that he works in Hollywood professionally as a Gaffer and Best Boy for IATSE Local 728. In 2012 he started Lucky Chicken Productions LLc to produce content with his friends. Beginning with just a few lights, Ryan continued to expand the lighting tools he had in inventory until friends and contacts started to rent from him for their own small productions. Today the company has the grip and lighting equipment for small and large scale productions, music videos and commercials. And while the established rental shops are reluctant to embrace technology, Ryan saw the need for smoother integration of client requests and inventory management using web applications.

When he started building web applications with a small team of amazing developers in 2015, Ryan quickly fell in love with the creative challenges. LCP's first iteration of an integrated rental site was built on a Lamp Stack with Angular v1 as the front-end and PHP as the back-end. The front-end experience is entirely Angular v1 and jQuery v2, but is not html rendered by Angular, instead twig templates render on the back-end because Angular templates are not indexable by Google due to their reliance on client-side AJAX. The front-end has views for both a public storefront and a custom private back-end which must be logged into. The custom back-end is mainly for inventory management like the adding of new inventory and the creation of corresponding product pages. Docusign is integrated for new user agreements and rental contracts built through the app, and Stripe is integrated for online payments via an e-commerce checkout. The back-end is PHP using the Silex web app framework from Symphony. For templating we are using the Twig Templating Engine. MySQL is used to store and retrieve the data using the PHP PDO library and a custom SQL query-building library. The system does not use a pre-built CMS, rather it is a custom CMS built upon the Silex web framework. Silex gives us the ability to hookup the twig render, MySQL, email and any other PHP libraries. The Silex framework handls all GET/POST/PUT/DELETE requests. Routing for these endpoints happens mainly on the Silex backend. Angular's $http module uses these to read/write data in the controllers. This combination of features is incredibly powerful and displays elegantly to the clients. Following several years of testing and improvement, the LCP team has made the decision to migrate our web application to the Mern Stack.
